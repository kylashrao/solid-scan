How to Get the Perfect Document Scan

Scanning physical paper using a mobile camera can sometimes result in shadows, warped text, or low contrast. To get the most out of SolidScan's local processing engine, follow these three steps for a perfect, professional PDF every time.

1. Maximize Natural Light

Avoid direct overhead indoor lights. Standard room lighting casts the shadow of your phone directly onto the center of the paper, creating an uneven gradient that is difficult to process. Instead, place your document near a window with indirect sunlight for perfectly even illumination.

2. Utilize the Dark Background Trick

When placing your document down, make sure the surface underneath it contrasts heavily with the paper. If you are scanning white paper, place it on a dark wood table or a black mat. This helps your camera's native auto-focus lock onto the crisp edges of the document instantly.

3. Leverage the SolidScan Contrast Engine

SolidScan features a mathematically tuned, client-side contrast engine. After capturing your raw image, click the "Enhance Contrast" button. Our HTML5 Canvas algorithm instantly brightens the page background and deepens the text ink, mimicking the output of a high-end mechanical flatbed scanner.

📘 How to Use SolidScan

1. Capture Your Document

Upload a file or use your camera to snap a photo. Click "Enhance Contrast" if the image needs to be clearer for better text extraction.

2. Process & Extract

Wait for the "Scan complete" message. Click on any thumbnail in the gallery strip to select that page; it will be highlighted with a border.

3. Copy & Save

Once a page is selected, click "📋 Copy Text" to save the content to your clipboard. Click "💾 Save as PDF" to bundle all your scans into one document.

Tip: For the best results, ensure your document is in a well-lit area and held steady!
